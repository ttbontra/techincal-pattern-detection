Pattern Overview

Name: Scallop Pattern

Type: Reversal/Continuation

Appearance: 'J' shape in uptrends for bullish signs, inverted 'J' in downtrends for bearish signs. Indicates a price direction change.

Significance: Signals sentiment shift; bullish in upward scallops, bearish in downward scallops.

Trading Strategy
Entry

Signal: Long on upward scallop's 'J' ascent, short on downward scallop's descent.

Confirmation: Trend line breakout or volume increase reinforces entry.

Exit

Target: Project pattern's height from breakout for profit target.

Stop Loss: Outside scallop's end: below for upward, above for downward.

Risk Management

Position Size: Keep potential loss to 1-2% of capital.

Profit Booking: Book partial profits with a trailing stop loss on remaining position.

Considerations

Volume: Volume spike on exit confirms pattern.

Pattern Recognition: Use technical tools due to the pattern's subjectivity.

Market Context: Consider overall market sentiment for better analysis.
