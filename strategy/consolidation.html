Pattern Overview

Name: Consolidation

Type: Continuation/Reversal

Appearance: Price moves in a confined range, showing supply-demand balance. Seen as flat tops/bottoms and forms rectangles, triangles, or pennants.

Significance: Indicates trend pause; breakout/breakdown suggests next move direction.

Trading Strategy
Entry

Signal: Trade on pattern breakout/breakdown with volume. For bullish, enter above the upper range; for bearish, below the lower range.

Confirmation: Confirm with a candle closing outside range to avoid false signals.

Exit

Target: Set target by the consolidation height added/subtracted from the breakout/breakdown point.

Stop Loss: Inside opposite consolidation boundary to limit losses.

Risk Management

Position Size: Adjust to keep potential loss at 1-2% of capital.

Profit Booking: Take partial profits at target, consider a trailing stop loss for remaining position.

Considerations

Volume: Volume spike on breakout/breakdown confirms pattern.

False Breakouts: Use confirmation and stop loss to manage false breakout risk.

Time Frame: Day traders focus on short time frames for quick trades.
