Pattern Overview

Name: Inverse Scallop Pattern

Type: Reversal or Continuation

Appearance: Opposite of Scallop. 'Upside-down J' in uptrends for bearish moves, 'J' in downtrends for bullish moves. Indicates gradual price direction change.

Significance: Suggests a sentiment shift, either bearish in uptrends or bullish in downtrends.

Trading Strategy
Entry

Signal: Short for 'upside-down J' when rounding top starts descending. Long for 'J' when curve starts ascending.

Confirmation: Breakout from trend line or increased volume as exit from scallop shape.

Exit

Target: Profit targets set by projecting the pattern's opening height from breakout. Downward for bearish, upward for bullish.

Stop Loss: Outside scallop formation to minimize losses; above for bearish, below for bullish.

Risk Management

Position Size: Adjust for max 1-2% capital risk.

Profit Booking: Book partial profits at targets; consider trailing stop loss for remaining position.

Considerations

Volume: Volume increase on breakout confirms pattern.

Pattern Recognition: Use technical tools for reliable trading signals due to pattern's subjective nature.

Market Context: Analyze within current market trends for effectiveness.
