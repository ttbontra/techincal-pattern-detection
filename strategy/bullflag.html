Pattern Overview

Name: Bull Flag Pattern

Type: Continuation

Appearance: Forms during an uptrend with a sharp rise ('pole') and a slight downward consolidation ('flag'). Completes with a breakout upwards.

Significance: Indicates uptrend continuation after consolidation, as bulls prepare for the next push.

Trading Strategy
Entry

Signal: Enter on a volume-supported breakout above the flag.

Confirmation: A candle closing above the flag may confirm a solid entry, reducing false breakout risks.

Exit

Target: Target equals the flagpole's length projected from the breakout point.

Stop Loss: Below the flag's lower trendline or recent swing low to limit losses.

Risk Management

Position Size: Adjust to risk no more than 1-2% of capital per trade.

Profit Booking: Book partial profits at levels or use a trailing stop to secure gains.

Considerations

Volume: Breakout volume spike strengthens pattern validity.

False Breakouts: Use confirmation and stop loss to mitigate risks.

Market Conditions: Trade bull flags in bullish markets; be cautious during bear markets or high volatility.
